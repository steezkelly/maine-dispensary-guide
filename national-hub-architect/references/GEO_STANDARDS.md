# 2026 SEO & GEO CONTENT STANDARDS

## 1. Information Gain (The Authority Metric)
Search engines in 2026 prioritize "Information Gain"—how much *new* information a page provides compared to existing results.
- **Rule:** Do not summarize existing content. Add one unique data point (Local X-Factor) that no competitor has.
- **Target:** Municipal fee schedules, specific town council procedures, or local zoning measurement quirks (e.g., "Entrance-to-Entrance").

## 2. GEO Engineering (Generative Engine Optimization)
GEO focuses on getting content cited by AI Search Engines (ChatGPT Search, Perplexity).
- **The "First 200 Words Strike":** The primary answer to the search intent must be delivered in a dense, factual box within the first 200 words.
- **Factual Density:** Maximize the number of verifiable facts per paragraph. Use tables for cost breakdowns and bulleted lists for requirements.

## 3. Structural Hierarchy
- **H1:** Primary Keyword (e.g., "Portland Dispensary Guide")
- **H2 (Fact Box):** Summary table of zoning, fees, and rules.
- **H2 (Narrative):** Strategic opportunity and local context.
- **H2 (Compliance):** Technical links to OCP and Maine Statutes.

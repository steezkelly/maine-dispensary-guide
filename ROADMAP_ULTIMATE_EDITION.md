## 📜 Chapter 1: The Founder's War Chest
*Strategy before Spending.*

### 1.1 The Budgetary Reality (2026 Tiers)
Don't guess at your numbers. The Maine market requires professional capitalization.
- **The Lean Bootstrap ($300k - $400k):** High focus on used equipment, smaller footprint, and "sweat equity" from the founders.
- **The Professional Entry ($500k - $700k):** Prime location, professional design, and 6 months of liquid working capital.
- **The Premium Flagship ($1M+):** Top-tier real estate (Portland/Old Port), custom millwork, and advanced lab-testing partnerships.

### 1.2 Funding Your Vision
- **Personal Capital:** Always the first $50k. It shows "Skin in the Game."
- **Debt Financing:** Use Maine-based credit unions. National banks will not touch the industry until federal legalization is final. 
- **Business Line of Credit:** Essential for asset protection. Never buy your initial inventory on a personal credit card.

## 📍 Chapter 3: Strategic Real Estate
*The Municipal Chess Match.*

### 3.1 The "Municipal Choice" Doctrine
In Maine, the state license is the "permission," but the town is the "gatekeeper." 
- **Opt-In vs Opt-Out:** Verify the town's vote status. Some towns allow cultivation but prohibit retail.
- **The "Buffer" Rule:** 1,000ft from schools is the state baseline. Be aware that "Entrance to Entrance" measurement is the OCP standard.

## ⚔️ Chapter 4: The OCP Gauntlet
*Winning your ticket to the market.*

### 4.1 The Three-Stage Process
Maine’s [Office of Cannabis Policy (OCP)](https://www.maine.gov/dafs/ocp/) does not issue licenses in a single burst. You must clear three distinct gates:
1.  **Conditional Licensure:** You are vetted as an individual and a business. (No premises needed yet).
2.  **Local Authorization:** You take your Conditional License to the town. This is where you pass local zoning and public hearings.
3.  **Active Licensure:** You prove to the state that your facility is built, secure, and ready for inspection.

## ⚖️ Chapter 6: The 280E Tax Fortress
*Defending your profits from the IRS.*

### 6.1 Understanding 280E
The Internal Revenue Code Section 280E prohibits cannabis businesses from deducting standard business expenses. You will pay taxes on your **Gross Profit**, not your Net Profit.
- **What is Deductible:** Only **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)**. 
- **The Strategy:** Work with a Maine cannabis CPA to properly categorize "Production Labor" and "Inventory Management" as COGS wherever legally possible.
